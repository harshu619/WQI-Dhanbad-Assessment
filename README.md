# WQI-Dhanbad-Assessment
Environmental Engineering project that assesses the Water Quality Index (WQI) of the Damodar River using the Weighted Arithmetic Index Method. Includes laboratory analysis, WQI calculations, graphical interpretation, and water quality classification based on BIS and CPCB standards.
💧 Water Quality Index (WQI) Assessment of the Damodar River, Dhanbad

📌 Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive Water Quality Index (WQI) Assessment of the Damodar River in the Dhanbad coal-mining region using the Weighted Arithmetic Index Method. Water samples were collected from three strategically selected locations to evaluate the impact of industrial discharge on river water quality.

The project integrates environmental engineering principles, laboratory analysis, and data interpretation to quantify water quality and classify it according to standard WQI categories.

---

🎯 Objective

- Assess the surface water quality of the Damodar River.
- Calculate the Water Quality Index (WQI) using the Weighted Arithmetic Index Method.
- Compare water quality across upstream, industrial discharge, and downstream sampling locations.
- Identify the major pollutants contributing to water quality deterioration.
- Provide recommendations for sustainable river water management.

---

🌍 Study Area

River: Damodar River

Location: Dhanbad, Jharkhand, India

Sampling Stations

- S1: Upstream
- S2: Near Industrial/Coal Washery Discharge
- S3: Downstream

---

🧪 Water Quality Parameters Analysed

- pH
- Dissolved Oxygen (DO)
- Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD)
- Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)
- Nitrate
- Chloride
- Total Hardness
- Fluoride

---

🛠 Laboratory Equipment

- Digital pH Meter
- Dissolved Oxygen Meter / Winkler's Titration Kit
- BOD Incubator
- TDS Meter
- UV–Visible Spectrophotometer
- Titration Apparatus
- Ion-Selective Electrode
- Standard Water Sampling Bottles

---

⚙ Methodology

1. Selection of three sampling stations.
2. Collection of grab water samples.
3. On-site measurement of pH and TDS.
4. Determination of DO using Winkler's method.
5. Measurement of BOD after 5-day incubation.
6. Laboratory analysis of Nitrate, Chloride, Total Hardness, and Fluoride.
7. Comparison with BIS 10500:2012 and CPCB standards.
8. Calculation of:
   - Unit Weight (Wₙ)
   - Quality Rating (Qₙ)
   - Water Quality Index (WQI)
9. Classification of water quality based on WQI.

---

📊 Results

Sampling Station| WQI| Water Quality
S1 – Upstream| 35.35| Good
S2 – Near Industrial Discharge| 61.59| Poor
S3 – Downstream| 48.06| Good

Key Findings

- Upstream water exhibited good quality.
- Water quality deteriorated significantly near the industrial discharge location.
- Downstream water showed partial recovery due to natural dilution.
- BOD, DO, and Fluoride were the major contributors to increased WQI.

💡 Recommendations

- Treat industrial effluents before discharge.
- Regularly monitor BOD and DO levels.
- Strengthen enforcement of CPCB discharge standards.
- Conduct seasonal WQI monitoring to evaluate temporal variations.
